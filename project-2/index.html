Getting Started with Haircutting: A Beginner's Guide By Brianna Mazur

Have you ever thought about learning to cut hair? It might sound challenging at first, but with practice and a few key techniques, anyone can learn the basics. Cutting hair is not just about trimming split ends; it’s about using creativity, patience, and skill to help people look and feel their best. Whether you’re dreaming of becoming a professional hairstylist or just want to learn a cool skill, cutting hair can be fun and rewarding!

Why Learn to Cut Hair?

Learning to cut hair gives you the chance to express your creativity and help people feel confident. When you’re a hairstylist, you get to work with people every day, understand what styles make them feel amazing, and learn to bring those styles to life. Plus, there are endless styles and techniques to explore. You’ll never get bored because there’s always something new to learn!

The Basics of Haircutting Tools

Before diving into techniques, it’s important to know the tools you’ll need. A few essentials include:

  • Shears – Use sharp scissors specifically made for cutting hair. They’re designed to give a clean cut without damaging the hair.
  • Clippers – Clippers are essential for short hairstyles and barber cuts. They make it easy to achieve clean lines and different lengths.
  • Comb – Combs help section and manage hair, especially if you’re trying to achieve a specific style.
  • Spray Bottle – Wetting the hair a little before cutting helps with accuracy.
  • Clips – These keep sections of hair out of the way as you work, making it easier to stay organized.
  • Hairbrush – A good hairbrush helps to detangle hair and smooth it out before you start cutting. Brushing also allows you to see the natural shape and movement of the hair.

Getting Started with Staple Techniques

It’s smart to start with a few basic cutting techniques:

  1. The Blunt Cut – This is the most straightforward cut, perfect for beginners. Here, the hair is cut straight across at the ends, giving a clean, even look.
  2. Layering – Layers add dimension to hair, making it look fuller and bouncier. To layer, you’ll lift sections of hair and cut at a slight angle, creating a “stacked” effect.
  3. Pixie Cut – This short hairstyle is trendy and edgy. The hair is cut close to the head and can be styled in various ways. It's a great way to learn how to work with short hair.
  4. Barber Cut – Learning basic barbering techniques, like fades or crew cuts, can be really fun. These cuts often involve using clippers and require precision to achieve clean lines.

When practicing, start small. Ask a friend or family member if you can practice with a simple trim or experiment on a mannequin head until you feel comfortable.

Tips for Beginner Hairstylists

  1. Be Patient – Cutting hair takes practice, so go slowly, and don’t worry if it doesn’t look perfect right away.
  2. Keep It Safe – Always make sure you’re using tools properly. Hold shears securely, and be careful around ears and necks.
  3. Listen to Your “Client” – Whether it’s a friend, family member, or someone else, take time to listen to what they want. Good communication is a big part of haircutting.

Have Fun and Keep Practicing!

Learning to cut hair is like learning any other skill—it takes practice. The more you cut, the better you’ll get. And who knows? Maybe this will be the start of a future in hairstyling! Just remember, you’re helping people look and feel their best, which is one of the coolest parts of the job.
